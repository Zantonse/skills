Just wrapped a 3-month proof of concept with a financial services company.

They came in wanting to replace SailPoint.

But when we dug in, the real problem wasn't the technology — it was that nobody actually owned access reviews. Six different teams were running them six different ways. No consistency. No accountability. Just people doing their best with no shared process.

Swapping the platform wouldn't have fixed that.

So we changed the scope. Instead of a full IGA replacement, we focused on Okta Identity Governance — a tighter, more targeted fit for where they actually were.

The tech configuration? That was the easy part.

The harder work was getting alignment on who owns what, what "done" looks like on a certification campaign, and how to stop six teams from doing six different things.

Lesson I keep relearning in this work:

**Governance problems are almost always people and process problems first. The technology just makes the dysfunction more visible.**

If you're evaluating IGA tooling right now — before you issue an RFP, ask who owns your access review process today. If you can't answer that clearly, no platform is going to save you.

Fix the process, then automate it.
